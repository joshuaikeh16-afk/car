Brief History of Anime

Anime started in Japan in the early 20th century, but the modern style we know today really took off after World War II.

  • 1917: First known Japanese animation (very short films)
  • 1960s: Osamu Tezuka (God of Manga) created Astro Boy → first big TV anime series
  • 1980s–90s: Anime exploded globally (Akira, Dragon Ball, Sailor Moon)
  • 2000s–now: Streaming (Crunchyroll, Netflix) made anime huge worldwide

The Big 3

These three long-running shonen series are called the "Big 3" because they dominated anime popularity in the 2000s and shaped modern shonen:

1. Naruto / Naruto Shippuden (2002–2017)

Ninja boy Naruto Uzumaki wants to become Hokage (village leader). Full of friendship, hard work, and epic battles.

2. Bleach (2004–2012, revival 2022–)

Ichigo Kurosaki becomes a Soul Reaper and fights evil spirits. Cool sword fights and deep lore.

3. One Piece (1999–ongoing)

Monkey D. Luffy wants to become Pirate King. Adventure, treasure, friendship — longest running anime ever.

Tips for New Anime Watchers

  • Start with shorter series: Death Note (37 eps), Attack on Titan (S1), Demon Slayer, My Hero Academia
  • Popular genres: Shonen (action/adventure), Shojo (romance/drama), Isekai (transported to another world), Slice of Life (everyday life)
  • Best legal sites: Crunchyroll, Netflix, HIDIVE, Amazon Prime, Disney+ (some anime)
  • Watch order: Always follow release order or watch guides on MyAnimeList.net
  • Sub vs Dub: Sub (Japanese audio + English subs) is usually closer to original vision. Dub is good if you prefer English voices
